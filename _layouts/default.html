More about my background

Previously, I worked with David Held at the CMU Robotics Institute on object-centric 3D representations for precise 6D spatial reasoning from multi-view RGBD task observation. There, I defended my MS in Robotics. Before that, I obtained my BASc from Engineering Science at the University of Toronto, majoring in Robotics. There, I worked on NLP with Frank Rudzicz at Vector Institute. During my early exposure to research, I worked with Eugenia Kumacheva on microfabrication and microfluidics to fabricate human cerebral microvascular systems with PDMS.

During undergrad, I also worked on FPGA software development at Altera (then acquired by Intel), motion planning & control with lattice graphs for AV at aUToronto. I co-founded UTMIST, and was part of WISE.

Research

I develop robotic systems that learn high-precision, contact-rich manipulation from human demonstrations and generalize to novel visual and behavioral settings with minimal adaptation. My research combines 3D scene representations and motion planning with imitation learning for efficient skill transfer from few examples.
